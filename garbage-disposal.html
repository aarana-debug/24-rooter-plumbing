About This Service

What We Do

A humming disposal that does not spin has a jammed grinding plate — often a quick fix using the hex key port underneath the unit. A disposal that makes no sound at all usually has a tripped reset button or a faulty outlet. A leaking disposal typically needs a new mounting gasket or splash guard.

When a disposal is beyond repair — grinding plate worn down, motor burned out, or casing cracked — we carry replacement units from the top brands and can swap yours out in a single visit. We also clear the drain line if the disposal has been pushing food waste into a slow or blocked drain.

Common Questions

Garbage Disposal FAQs

My disposal hums but won't spin — is it dead?
Not necessarily. A humming disposal usually has a jammed grinding plate. Try pressing the reset button on the bottom of the unit and use the hex wrench port to manually free the plate. If it still hums, call us — the motor may need replacement.
How long should a garbage disposal last?
A quality disposal lasts 10 to 15 years with normal use. If yours is older than that and having problems, replacement is almost always more cost-effective than repair.
What should I never put down a disposal?
Avoid fibrous foods (celery, artichokes), starchy foods (potato peels, pasta, rice), grease, and hard items like bones or fruit pits. These cause jams and premature wear on the grinding plate.
