About This Service

What We Do

We handle everything from a simple toilet unclogging to a complete toilet replacement with new wax ring, supply line, and shut-off valve. If your toilet is running constantly, the flapper, fill valve, or flush valve is likely to blame — all repairs we can diagnose and fix in a single visit.

If your toilet leaks at the base, a failed wax ring seal is the likely culprit. Left unattended, this causes water damage to the subfloor and can require costly structural repairs. We pull, reset, and re-seal toilets quickly and correctly.

Common Questions

Toilet Repair FAQs

Why is my toilet running constantly?
A constantly running toilet is almost always caused by a worn flapper that is no longer sealing the flush valve, or a faulty fill valve that is not shutting off. Both are quick, inexpensive repairs.
Should I repair or replace my old toilet?
If your toilet is more than 20 years old and needs repeated repairs, replacement with a modern low-flow toilet (1.28 gpf) will pay for itself in water savings within a few years. We will give you an honest recommendation.
Is a toilet leak at the base serious?
Yes — a leak at the base means the wax ring seal has failed and sewer water is seeping onto the floor each time you flush. This damages the subfloor quickly. Call us right away.
