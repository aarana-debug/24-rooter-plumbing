About This Service

What We Do

Hydro jetting is the most thorough drain cleaning method available. A specialized nozzle propels high-pressure water in all directions as it moves through the pipe, scouring every inch of the interior wall. The result is a pipe that flows like new — not just one with a hole punched through the clog.

This method is especially effective for commercial kitchens where grease buildup is a constant issue, and for older Southern California homes where clay sewer lines have years of accumulated scale and root intrusion. We always perform a camera inspection first to confirm the pipe can safely handle jetting pressure.

Common Questions

Hydro Jetting FAQs

Is hydro jetting safe for old pipes?
We always camera-inspect pipes before jetting. If a pipe has significant damage or corrosion, we will recommend repair or replacement instead of jetting to avoid making the problem worse.
How is hydro jetting different from snaking?
Snaking is a mechanical auger that breaks through a clog. Hydro jetting uses high-pressure water to completely scour the pipe walls, removing all buildup that snaking leaves behind.
How often should a restaurant do hydro jetting?
Most commercial kitchens benefit from hydro jetting every 6 to 12 months to prevent grease accumulation. High-volume operations may need it more frequently.
