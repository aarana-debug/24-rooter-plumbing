About This Service

What We Do

Slab leaks are one of the most damaging plumbing failures a Southern California homeowner can face. The post-war homes throughout the Pomona Valley were built on concrete slabs with copper pipes running directly underneath — and those pipes corrode over time. A pinhole slab leak can erode the soil beneath your foundation without any visible sign until major damage has occurred.

We use listening equipment and electronic amplifiers to hear the exact location of the leak, then make the smallest possible opening to access and repair it. We also detect leaks in walls, ceilings, irrigation systems, and underground service lines.

Common Questions

Leak Detection FAQs

How do I know if I have a slab leak?
Warning signs include a hot spot on the floor, the sound of running water when nothing is on, a sudden spike in your water bill, or cracks appearing in your flooring or walls. Call us immediately if you notice any of these — slab leaks worsen rapidly.
Will you have to break my concrete slab to fix it?
Not necessarily. Depending on the location and severity, we may be able to re-route the affected line through the wall or ceiling, completely bypassing the slab. We will give you all options and their costs upfront.
Does homeowner's insurance cover slab leaks?
Many homeowner policies cover the cost of detecting and accessing the leak, and some cover the repair itself. We provide detailed documentation to help you file a claim if applicable.
