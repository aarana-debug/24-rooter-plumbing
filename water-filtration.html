About This Service

What We Do

Claremont and the surrounding Inland Valley communities receive water with very high mineral content — primarily calcium and magnesium — that registers as "hard" water. This mineral content deposits as scale inside pipes, water heaters, dishwashers, washing machines, and fixtures, reducing efficiency and service life across your entire home.

A whole-home water softener addresses this at the point of entry, treating all the water coming into your house before it reaches any fixture or appliance. We also install sediment pre-filters, carbon filters for chlorine reduction, and under-sink reverse osmosis systems for drinking water.

Common Questions

Water Filtration FAQs

How hard is the water in Claremont and the Inland Valley?
Very hard. Municipal water in this region typically measures 15 to 25 grains per gallon (GPG) — anything above 7 GPG is considered hard. This causes rapid scale buildup in water heaters and pipes, and is a leading cause of premature appliance failure in SoCal homes.
Will a water softener affect my water pressure?
A properly sized and installed water softener has negligible impact on water pressure. In fact, by preventing scale buildup in pipes and fixtures, softened water often improves flow over time.
What is the difference between a water softener and a water filter?
A water softener removes calcium and magnesium ions that cause hardness. A water filter removes particles, sediment, chlorine, and other contaminants. Many homeowners benefit from both — a softener at the main line entry and a carbon or RO filter for drinking water.
