About This Service

What We Do

Southern California's older neighborhoods are full of clay and cast-iron sewer lines that are past their service life. Root intrusion, pipe bellying, corrosion, and joint separation all cause the same result: sewage backing up into your home. We start every sewer job with a camera inspection so you know exactly what you are dealing with before any work begins.

Depending on the condition and location of the line, we can often repair or replace the pipe with minimal excavation using trenchless methods. For lines that need traditional dig-and-replace, we do the work cleanly and restore the surface when we are done.

Common Questions

Sewer Line FAQs

How do I know if my sewer line needs to be replaced?
Multiple slow drains, gurgling toilets, sewage odors, or recurrent clogs despite repeated cleaning are signs of a failing sewer line. A camera inspection tells you the exact condition so you can make an informed decision.
What is trenchless sewer repair?
Trenchless methods allow us to replace or reline a sewer pipe from two small access points, without excavating the entire line. This protects your landscaping, driveway, and foundation and is often faster and less expensive than full excavation.
How long does sewer line replacement take?
A trenchless replacement typically takes one to two days. Traditional excavation varies by the length and depth of the line, but most residential sewer replacements are completed within two to four days.
