About This Service

What We Do

A whole-home repipe replaces all the supply lines in your house with new copper or PEX piping. PEX (cross-linked polyethylene) is flexible, freeze-resistant, and significantly less expensive to install than copper — making it the most popular choice today. Copper remains the gold standard for longevity and is preferred in some municipalities.

We route new pipes with minimal wall damage, patch and texture any access holes, and restore your water service the same day in most cases. The difference in water pressure and clarity is immediately noticeable.

Common Questions

Repiping FAQs

How do I know if my home needs repiping?
Low water pressure throughout the house, discolored or rust-tinted water, frequent pipe leaks in different locations, or having galvanized or polybutylene pipes installed are all strong indicators that a full repipe will be more cost-effective than continued patching.
Will I be without water during the repipe?
Water is shut off during active work but restored each evening. In most homes we complete the repipe in one to two days so disruption is minimal. We schedule work to match your household's needs.
Copper or PEX — which is better?
Both are excellent. PEX is flexible, more affordable, and easier to install in tight spaces. Copper is extremely durable and preferred by some homeowners and municipalities. We will walk you through the trade-offs and costs of each before you decide.
